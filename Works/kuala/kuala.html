回 kuala


2022–
Experimental print and Moving image








How do we photograph our memories?

These images are varied attempts to photograph my childhood memory of a landmark in my father’s hometown. I was too young back then to take any photographs. Working with 66 found images, the only available photographs of the landmark, I used various processes to erode these pictures in hopes of revealing the image of my memory.


印象深刻的茶壶雕塑在我记忆中是朦胧的。它是跟着我爸爸回汶莱的路上一定会经过的地标。

回汶莱一直是件很矛盾的事。我根本没有真的在文莱住过，又怎么回得去呢？我对文莱的记忆似乎好像一部少了很多镜头的电影，靠着亲戚们的旧照片和故事填充。

回忆里的茶壶有些怪异，它很大、很旧也有点滑稽。我记得立体图文、青苔和霉菌点缀着壶腹，却对路上的风景一点印象也没有。小时候没有手机也没有相机，所以我并没有茶壶的照片，靠的仅是回忆中的一些画面。

我等待回忆中的画面沉淀，我尝试把模糊的画面擦清楚、放大、截图、修改。回忆里的画面有时漂泊于他人所拍的照片中，但这些和脑海里所看见的还是有差距，它们最多只是一些印象和影子。图像库有再多的图片也只能是雾里看花，水中望月。越卖力想弄清楚回忆里的画面，它会变得越模糊和产生越多裂缝。

我记得最后一次看到茶壶应该有二十年前了，听说茶壶的装饰灯已经坏了，时钟也停了。


I recall the teapot vividly as it was the main landmark of the journey back to my dad’s childhood home in Brunei.

In my memory, the teapot looked garish and odd on its sprawling buttresses. I remember seeing it lit like a bright ball of tungsten, though I’m unsure if I’ve ever seen it in the dark. I remember seeing mould and gunk on it, but not the roads leading to the teapot roundabout or the house.

With every recount of the teapot roundabout, I feel the images in my head fracturing more. I search for its likeness in the photographs of others, clarifying, aggregating, and reframing it. They all look familiar but not quite right. The images in my memory wane the more I try to look at them.

I remember seeing the teapot over two decades ago, and rumor has it that the lights and clocks on it have already stopped working a few years ago.

This is a work in progress.
