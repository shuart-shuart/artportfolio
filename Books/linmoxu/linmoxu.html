临摹 – 序 (linmo – sketches)


2020
French-fold booklet, printed on Newsprint
24 x 17 x 1cm, 68pp
self-published


















This zine was conceived as a shorthand for charting the process-led investigation for 临摹 Lapping Emulations.

The book adopts a visually heavy narrative strategy. The collaged images and texts provide glimpses of the various visual experimentations and research surrounding Shu's inquiry into her identity as a Henghua person in the Singapore Chinese diaspora. Three key texts pace the book, expressing the nuances and complexities of an unstable and protean cultural identity.

The research process gathers scholarly and literature texts surrounding the Chinese diaspora in Singapore and Southeast Asia while comparing that with personal and familial histories. In response, Shu conjures new manners of image-making. Her improvised darkroom and editing techniques foster cycles of relooking. This was potent for rumination and holding onto ideas for a liminal identity and culture. The sea became this rich allegory and visual mine. Like the sea, Chineseness is never stagnant but always an amorphous entity of shifting formless bodies.

Borrowing the words of Kuo Pao Kun, linmo – sketches is just the journal of another “cultural orphan".
