花·瓶·画 flower, vases, paintings and other observations


2025
accordion book
self-published


















Flowers, porcelain vases, and scroll paintings are common elements in the studio sets of erhu music videos.

Drawn from both official and amateur recordings, these images have been endlessly reposted, their details fading with each cycle of compression and duplication. What remains are ghostly echoes of once-pristine performances, now softened by time and the hands that have passed them along.

Many of these videos, mostly from the 1980s and 1990s, have been reposted and copied so many times that their quality has deteriorated. Details blur, colors fade, and compression artifacts settle in—yet these worn images continue to circulate and are emulated.

In this study, these observations are brought together and the fragments are reorganised as a way to look at how repetition and digital decay shape our impressions of a “Chinese music” look. Rather than seeing them as mere traces of the past, it considers how certain motifs persist, and how they take on new meaning over time.

花·瓶·画 flower, vases, paintings and other observations was as part of Feng Shui in the Expanded Field during Still Rendering: Projects by the 2024-25 Design Research Fellows presented by Singapore Art Museum, Design Collection.
The book will be available at Singapore Art Book Fair 2025.
